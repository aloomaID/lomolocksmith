Types of Home Locks

Choosing the right home lock is essential for safeguarding your property. At Lomo Locksmith, we provide expert locksmith services across all cities in Columbine Colorado offering a wide range of locks to meet your security needs. Here’s a quick guide to the most common types of home locks.
Deadbolt Locks: Deadbolts are the gold standard for home security, providing a strong barrier against forced entry. Available in single and double-cylinder variants, deadbolts are a must-have for exterior doors. They offer superior protection thanks to their solid, heavy-duty construction.
Smart Locks: For those seeking convenience and high-tech security, smart locks are a perfect choice. With keyless entry, remote access, and integration with smart home systems, these locks offer advanced security features. You can control access to your home from your smartphone, making it easy to monitor and manage who enters your property.
Knob Locks: Typically used in conjunction with deadbolts, knob locks offer basic security. They are common on interior doors but should not be relied on solely for exterior doors due to their vulnerability to forced entry.
Lever Handle Locks: Ideal for interior doors, lever handle locks are user-friendly, especially for those with mobility issues. While they are easy to operate, they are best used in low-security areas of the home.
Cam Locks: Commonly found on cabinets, mailboxes, and other less secure areas, cam locks are great for securing small spaces. They offer an extra layer of security where needed.
At Lomo Locksmith, we help you select and install the best locks for your home, ensuring peace of mind and enhanced security. Contact us today to explore the best locking solutions for your needs in Columbine Colorado .

Should I Rekey or Replace My Locks?

Deciding between rekeying or replacing your locks can be a tough call for homeowners in Columbine Colorado . At Lomo Locksmith, we understand the importance of making the right choice for your security needs. Both rekeying and replacing locks offer distinct benefits, but knowing which option is best for your situation is key to keeping your home safe.
Rekeying Your Locks
Rekeying involves changing the internal pins of your existing locks, making old keys useless while allowing you to use a new key. This option is ideal if you’ve recently moved into a new home, lost your keys, or want to prevent unauthorized access without altering your door hardware. Rekeying is cost-effective and quick, offering an immediate solution to security concerns.
Replacing Your Locks
Replacing your locks involves installing new hardware altogether, which is the best choice if your current locks are old, damaged, or you want to upgrade to a more secure option like deadbolts or smart locks. It’s also recommended if your locks have been tampered with or compromised. Replacing locks provides an opportunity to enhance your home’s overall security with modern features.
Which Option Is Right for You?
If your locks are in good condition and you’re simply concerned about key control, rekeying is a great, affordable choice. However, if you’re looking to upgrade or if the locks are damaged, replacing them is the way to go.
