Types of Home Locks

Choosing the right home lock is essential for safeguarding your property. At Lomo Locksmith, we provide expert locksmith services across all cities in Grandview Plaza Kansas offering a wide range of locks to meet your security needs. Here’s a quick guide to the most common types of home locks.
Deadbolt Locks: Deadbolts are the gold standard for home security, providing a strong barrier against forced entry. Available in single and double-cylinder variants, deadbolts are a must-have for exterior doors. They offer superior protection thanks to their solid, heavy-duty construction.
Smart Locks: For those seeking convenience and high-tech security, smart locks are a perfect choice. With keyless entry, remote access, and integration with smart home systems, these locks offer advanced security features. You can control access to your home from your smartphone, making it easy to monitor and manage who enters your property.
Knob Locks: Typically used in conjunction with deadbolts, knob locks offer basic security. They are common on interior doors but should not be relied on solely for exterior doors due to their vulnerability to forced entry.
Lever Handle Locks: Ideal for interior doors, lever handle locks are user-friendly, especially for those with mobility issues. While they are easy to operate, they are best used in low-security areas of the home.
Cam Locks: Commonly found on cabinets, mailboxes, and other less secure areas, cam locks are great for securing small spaces. They offer an extra layer of security where needed.
At Lomo Locksmith, we help you select and install the best locks for your home, ensuring peace of mind and enhanced security. Contact us today to explore the best locking solutions for your needs in Grandview Plaza Kansas .

Should I Rekey or Replace My Locks?

Deciding whether to rekey or replace your locks can be challenging, especially when home security is a priority. At Lomo Locksmith, serving all of Grandview Plaza Kansas we help homeowners make informed decisions about their locks. Understanding the difference between rekeying and replacing locks can save you time, money, and ensure the security of your home.
Rekeying Your Locks
Rekeying is a cost-effective way to enhance your home’s security without changing the entire lock. This process involves altering the internal mechanism of the existing lock so that a new key works while the old one does not. Rekeying is ideal if you've lost your keys, moved into a new home, or experienced a security breach. It’s quicker, more affordable, and offers the peace of mind you need without a full lock replacement.
Replacing Your Locks
Replacing locks is a more comprehensive solution that involves installing new lock hardware. This option is perfect when upgrading to a more secure system, switching to smart locks, or if your current locks are damaged or outdated. Replacing locks allows you to choose from a variety of styles and security levels, providing a fresh start with maximum protection.
Which Option is Best?
Rekeying is best when your locks are in good condition but require a security update. However, if you need improved security or want to upgrade, replacing your locks is the way to go.
