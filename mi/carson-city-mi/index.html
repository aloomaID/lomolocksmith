Types of Home Locks

Choosing the right home lock is essential for safeguarding your property. At Lomo Locksmith, we provide expert locksmith services across all cities in Carson City Michigan offering a wide range of locks to meet your security needs. Here’s a quick guide to the most common types of home locks.
Deadbolt Locks: Deadbolts are the gold standard for home security, providing a strong barrier against forced entry. Available in single and double-cylinder variants, deadbolts are a must-have for exterior doors. They offer superior protection thanks to their solid, heavy-duty construction.
Smart Locks: For those seeking convenience and high-tech security, smart locks are a perfect choice. With keyless entry, remote access, and integration with smart home systems, these locks offer advanced security features. You can control access to your home from your smartphone, making it easy to monitor and manage who enters your property.
Knob Locks: Typically used in conjunction with deadbolts, knob locks offer basic security. They are common on interior doors but should not be relied on solely for exterior doors due to their vulnerability to forced entry.
Lever Handle Locks: Ideal for interior doors, lever handle locks are user-friendly, especially for those with mobility issues. While they are easy to operate, they are best used in low-security areas of the home.
Cam Locks: Commonly found on cabinets, mailboxes, and other less secure areas, cam locks are great for securing small spaces. They offer an extra layer of security where needed.

Should I Rekey or Replace My Locks?

When faced with a security concern, many homeowners in Carson City Michigan wonder whether they should rekey or replace their locks. At Lomo Locksmith, we understand that making the right choice is essential for your peace of mind and safety.
Rekeying Your Locks
Rekeying involves altering the internal mechanism of your existing locks so that old keys no longer work. This is a cost-effective solution, especially if you’ve lost your keys, moved into a new home, or experienced a security breach. Rekeying is ideal when your locks are still in good condition but you need to ensure that only you and trusted individuals have access to your home. It's a quick and affordable way to enhance security without replacing the entire lock system.
Replacing Your Locks
Replacing your locks, on the other hand, is recommended when your locks are outdated, damaged, or compromised. This option provides an opportunity to upgrade to more secure and modern locks, such as deadbolts or smart locks. Replacing locks is essential if you've experienced a break-in or if you’re looking to improve the overall security of your property in Carson City Michigan . Carson City, MI Insights: Population, Influence, and Service Areas

Carson City was first platted in 1866 on land owned by R. M. Abbott, Delia Miner, and Hiram T. Sherman and recorded in 1871. The first lot was sold to Thomas Scott and two nephews, John and Thomas LaDue in 1867. With the corporate name Scott & LaDue, they built a sawmill in 1868 and a grist mill in 1870. The post office from North Shade in Gratiot County was transferred to this location on August 3, 1868, with Hiram T. Sherman as the first postmaster. The name Carson City was given to the post office by Thomas Scott, who had been in Carson City, Nevada, during the gold and silver mining boom days. Carson City incorporated as a village in 1887 and as a city in 1960. The first mayor of Carson City was John T Hallett.
